# FTIR Analysis - Sample Nature Assessment

## Executive Summary

Based on comprehensive FTIR spectroscopy analysis, the three bacterial samples show distinct biochemical profiles with significant differences in composition, complexity, and potential heavy metal interactions.

---

## Sample 1 (Bacteria 1) - Most Complex Profile

### Biochemical Composition:

**Proteins (High Content):**
- **Amide I (Proteins)** - Strong signal at ~1633 cm⁻¹
- **Amide II (Proteins)** - Strong signal at ~1548 cm⁻¹
- **C-N Stretching (Amines)** - Indicates protein backbone and amino groups

**Interpretation:** Sample 1 has the **highest protein content** among all three samples. The strong Amide I and Amide II bands suggest:
- Abundant protein biomass
- Possible different growth phase (exponential vs stationary)
- Different bacterial species or strain
- Higher metabolic activity

**Lipids:**
- **C-H Bending (Lipids)** - Detected
- **C-H Stretching (Lipids/Proteins)** - Present in membrane lipids

**Interpretation:** Moderate lipid content, typical of bacterial cell membranes.

**Nucleic Acids:**
- **P=O Stretching (Phosphates)** - Indicates nucleic acids (DNA/RNA)
- **PO2- Symmetric Stretching** - Confirms nucleic acid presence

**Interpretation:** Nucleic acids present, indicating active cellular material.

**Aromatic Compounds:**
- **C-H Bending (Aromatic)** - Multiple peaks
- **C=C Stretching (Aromatic)** - Aromatic ring structures

**Interpretation:** Aromatic compounds suggest:
- Aromatic amino acids (Phe, Tyr, Trp) in proteins
- Possible secondary metabolites
- Complex organic structures

**Sulfur-Containing Compounds:**
- **C-S Stretching (Thiols)** - Unique to Sample 1

**Interpretation:** Sulfur-containing biomolecules present:
- Cysteine residues in proteins
- Possible thiol groups in enzymes
- Unique biochemical signature

**Water/Alcohol:**
- **O-H Stretching (Water/Alcohols)** - Strong, broad band

**Interpretation:** High water content or alcohol groups:
- Possible different sample preparation
- Higher hydration state
- Alcohol-containing metabolites

**Heavy Metals Detected:**
- **Hg-S Stretching (Mercury)** - Multiple peaks (403, 452, 489 cm⁻¹)
- **Cr-O Stretching (Chromium)** - Detected at ~534 cm⁻¹
- **As-O Stretching (Arsenic)** - Detected at ~765 cm⁻¹

**Interpretation:** Sample 1 shows **multiple heavy metal interactions**:
- Mercury binding (possibly to sulfur groups - explains C-S stretching)
- Chromium contamination or resistance
- Arsenic presence
- **Most contaminated sample** or **highest metal resistance**

### Overall Nature of Sample 1:
- **Most biochemically complex** (16 peaks vs 9 in others)
- **Highest protein content**
- **Unique sulfur-containing compounds**
- **Multiple heavy metal interactions**
- **Possible different species** or **different growth conditions**
- **Higher metabolic activity** or **stress response**

## Heavy Metal Analysis

- **Interpretation:** Mercury is likely present in growth medium or environment

### Chromium (Cr):
- **Present in Samples 1 & 3** (not in Sample 2)
- **Interpretation:** Variable contamination or different resistance mechanisms

### Arsenic (As):

- **Interpretation:** Sample 1 may have higher arsenic tolerance or different source

### Overall Metal Profile:
- **Sample 1:** Most contaminated (3 metals) or most resistant
- **Sample 2:** Least contaminated (1 metal)
- **Sample 3:** Moderate contamination (2 metals)
